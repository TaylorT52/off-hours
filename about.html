About / Mission

Build with technical women.

Off Hours is a community for women who want to build a real technical network through projects, collaboration, and consistent time spent making things together.

We believe the most interesting ideas come from building with ambitious, thoughtful people, whether that leads to a side project, a lasting collaboration, or something larger over time.

What Off Hours is for

The goal is to make it easier to meet other technical women, find collaborators you want to work with, and turn interesting ideas into projects you can actually build together.

At its core, Off Hours is about creating the kind of informal builder network that already seems to form naturally in some startup circles: people pulling each other into projects, trading feedback quickly, and learning by making side by side.

What we care about

  • interesting ideas
  • building together
  • finding people you want to keep building with

Why This Exists

What feels missing right now.

The gap

It often feels like there is a more natural culture among technical men of building together, pulling friends into projects, and constantly sharing what they are working on. We want to make that same kind of builder community feel more real and accessible for women.

The difference

A lot of existing groups feel either broad or purely founder-oriented. We want something more project-based, technical, and builder-led.

Our thesis

Real ideas usually come from building with interesting people, not from trying to force them in isolation. Better technical communities create better projects, stronger teams, and more chances for ambitious work to actually take shape.

How It Works

A builder network with projects at the center.

Build nights

Small gatherings where people share what they are making, brainstorm new ideas, and find collaborators naturally.

Working sessions

Office hours and workshop-style days where people actually sit down, make progress, and help each other through technical problems.

Pathways to momentum

Over time, the goal is to create real pathways from early curiosity to stronger projects: more technical confidence, more ambitious collaborations, and eventually things worth sharing at demo days, showcases, or with sponsors.
